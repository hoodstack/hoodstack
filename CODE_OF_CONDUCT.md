# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in HoodStack a harassment-free experience for
everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Standards

Behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- being respectful of differing opinions and experiences
- giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- accepting responsibility, apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and
  learning from them
- focusing on what is best for the community
- being direct about technical disagreement without making it personal

Unacceptable behaviour:

- sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- public or private harassment
- publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## A note specific to this project

HoodStack is financial infrastructure. Two things follow.

**Report problems, including embarrassing ones.** If you find that something we
claim is not true - a security property that does not hold, a capability that
does not work - say so. That is a contribution, not an attack, and it will be
treated as one.

**Do not pressure anyone to overstate what works.** Accuracy about our own
capabilities is a safety property. Requesting that a maintainer describe an
unfinished feature as finished, or an unaudited system as audited, is a conduct
issue.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behaviour to **conduct@hoodstack.io**. All complaints will be
reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and the privacy and security of
the reporter will be respected.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the
project's leadership.

## Enforcement guidelines

1. **Correction** - private written warning, with clarity about the violation.
2. **Warning** - consequences for continued behaviour; no interaction with those
   involved for a specified period.
3. **Temporary ban** - from any interaction or public communication with the
   community.
4. **Permanent ban** - from any public interaction within the community.
